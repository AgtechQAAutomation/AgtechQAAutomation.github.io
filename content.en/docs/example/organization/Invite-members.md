## Invite Members

The **Invite Members** feature allows organization administrators to onboard new users into the RAITHU platform. This ensures that the right people have access to the organization with clearly defined roles and responsibilities.

Inviting users through this workflow maintains secure access control while enabling teams to scale operations efficiently across regions and functions.

---

### Accessing Invite Members

1. Log in to RAITHU with an authorized account.
2. Navigate to **User Management → View Users**.
3. Click **Invite Members** to open the invitation panel.

The invitation form appears on the right side of the screen.

---

### Invitation Form Fields

To invite a new member, provide the following details:

- **First Name**  
  Enter the first name of the user being invited.

- **Last Name**  
  Enter the last name of the user being invited.

- **Phone Number**  
  Provide the user’s contact number for identification and communication purposes.

- **Email Address**  
  Enter the official work email address of the user. An invitation will be sent to this email.

- **Role**  
  Select the appropriate role for the user (for example: **Admin, Owner , Member** ).  
  The assigned role determines the user’s access level and permissions within the organization.

---

### Sending an Invitation

After filling in all required fields, click **Send Invite**.  
The invited user will receive an email with instructions to accept the invitation and complete account setup.

Until the invitation is accepted, the user will appear in the user list with an **Invite Sent** status. Once accepted, the status updates to **Accepted**, and the user gains access based on their assigned role.

---

### Why Inviting Members Matters

The Invite Members feature helps organizations:
- Maintain secure and controlled access  
- Assign clear roles and responsibilities  
- Manage large field and supervisory teams efficiently  
- Ensure accountability across operations  

The next sections of this manual explain role definitions, permission levels, and how to manage users after they have been onboarded.
